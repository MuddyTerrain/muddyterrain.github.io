<h2>Using Google Gemini in Unreal Engine</h2>

<p>Google's Gemini models bring something unique to the table for game developers: strong multimodal capabilities, competitive pricing, and features like native image generation and TTS baked right into the model family.</p>

<h2>What Makes Gemini Interesting for Games</h2>

<p><strong>Multimodal input.</strong> Gemini can process both text and images in a single request. Feed it a screenshot of your game and ask it to describe what's happening, generate commentary, or make decisions based on visual context. This opens the door to AI spectator systems, visual puzzle solvers, and environment-aware NPCs.</p>

<p><strong>Built-in image generation.</strong> Gemini's Nano Banana models generate images directly — no separate DALL-E call needed. Useful for in-game concept art, dynamic textures, or visual content that adapts to player actions.</p>

<p><strong>Native TTS.</strong> Gemini can generate spoken audio directly, with a wide selection of voices and language support. One model family handling text, images, and voice simplifies your integration significantly.</p>

<p><strong>Realtime audio.</strong> For truly interactive voice-driven gameplay, Gemini's realtime endpoints support live conversational AI with low latency.</p>

<h2>Gemini Models Available</h2>

<p>The full Gemini lineup is supported:</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Gemini 3.1 Pro/Flash Lite</strong> — Latest generation with improved reasoning</li>
    <li><strong>Gemini 2.5 Pro/Flash</strong> — Excellent balance of capability and speed, with reasoning support</li>
    <li><strong>Imagen 4.0</strong> — Dedicated high-quality image generation</li>
    <li><strong>Gemini TTS</strong> — Native text-to-speech</li>
</ul>
