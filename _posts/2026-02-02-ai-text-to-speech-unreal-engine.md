<h2>AI Text to Speech for Unreal Engine Game Characters</h2>

<p>Voice acting is one of the most expensive and time-consuming parts of game development. AI text-to-speech is changing that — not by replacing voice actors entirely, but by making voiced dialogue accessible to indie teams and enabling use cases that traditional recording simply can't handle.</p>

<h2>Where AI TTS Makes Sense</h2>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Dynamic dialogue.</strong> If your NPCs generate their lines with AI, you can't pre-record them. TTS turns AI-generated text into spoken audio in real-time.</li>
    <li><strong>Prototyping.</strong> Test your dialogue system with actual voice before committing to professional recording sessions. Find pacing issues, awkward lines, and tonal mismatches early.</li>
    <li><strong>Procedural content.</strong> Thousands of generated item descriptions, quest briefings, or lore entries that would be impractical to record manually.</li>
    <li><strong>Localization.</strong> Generate voiced dialogue in dozens of languages without hiring voice talent for each one.</li>
    <li><strong>Accessibility.</strong> Give visually impaired players full access to text-heavy content.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The Providers</h2>

<p>The quality of AI voices has improved dramatically. Here's what's available for Unreal Engine today:</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>OpenAI TTS</strong> — Clean, natural-sounding voices with streaming support. Great for general dialogue.</li>
    <li><strong>ElevenLabs</strong> — Industry-leading voice quality with 50+ voices and extensive language support. Ideal when voice quality is the top priority.</li>
    <li><strong>Inworld AI</strong> — Purpose-built for game characters, with models designed specifically for interactive entertainment.</li>
    <li><strong>Google Gemini TTS</strong> — Native TTS within the Gemini model family, supporting a huge roster of voices and languages.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Streaming Matters</h2>

<p>The difference between a 2-second delay and instant speech is the difference between immersion and frustration. Audio streaming delivers the first syllables while the rest is still being generated — your characters start talking immediately.</p>
